HISTORY

A Story Spanning Two Millennia

Rajgadh Palace and its surrounding settlements originated as a riverine fortified hub around c. 200 CE. Over centuries the site grew into a political, religious and artisanal center, shaped by successive dynasties, trade routes, and spiritual movements.

LIVING CULTURE

Arts, Festivals & Community Life

Rajgadh is not just monuments â€“ it is a living hub of performance, craft and ritual. Generations of families maintain weaving, bronze casting, stone-carving and storytelling traditions. Seasonal festivals animate the palace courtyards and river ghats.

Traditional Dance â€“ Nalini Nritya
Floor-based narrative dance performed at dawn during the harvest festival. Costumes use handwoven textiles and natural dyes from local plants.
Bronze & Metalwork Guilds
Continuous lineage of bronze casting using lost-wax technique. Guild workshops produce ritual icons, bells and contemporary commissions.
Weaving & Textiles
Multi-shaft loom weaving producing ikat and tapestry panels, traditionally woven by women's cooperatives for temple festivals and market trade.
Oral Storytellers (Katha)
Professional storytellers who recite local history and moral tales in the evening assembly on the palace steps.
