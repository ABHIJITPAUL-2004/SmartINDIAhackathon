Upcoming Cultural Events

Traditional Dance Festival

Traditional dance festivals are vibrant annual celebrations of cultural heritage that feature diverse performances, often set against historic backdrops. These festivals serve to preserve traditions and foster cultural exchange by bringing together artists and audiences from around the world.

Khajuraho Dance Festival (India)
Description: A week-long festival of classical Indian dance held in February or March. It is organized by the Madhya Pradesh Kala Parishad to promote Indian classical dance forms.
Setting: Performances take place in front of the Chitragupta and Vishwanatha Temples, with the illuminated Khajuraho temples forming a spectacular backdrop.
Dance forms: Bharatanatyam, Kathak, Odissi, Kuchipudi, Manipuri, Kathakali, and modern dance.

Konark Dance Festival (India)
Description: A five-day carnival of classical Indian dance in December, started in 1986 by Odisha Tourism.
Setting: Held against the backdrop of the 13th-century Sun Temple in Konark, Odisha.
Dance forms: Odissi, Bharatanatyam, Manipuri, Kathakali, Kathak, and Sattriya.

Andong International MaskDance Festival (South Korea)
Description: A week-long festival in September at Andong Hahoe Folk Village.
Highlights: Korean mask dance, global folk performances, parades, workshops, and competitions.

Hornbill Festival (Nagaland, India)
Description: Known as the "Festival of Festivals," held every December near Kohima.
Highlights: Folk dances, music, craft exhibitions, food stalls, traditional sports, and concerts.

International Folklore Festival Beltinci (Slovenia)
Description: A four-day July festival at Beltinci Castle & Park.
Features: Folkloric dance, traditional customs, folk music, and art exhibitions.

Folklorama (Canada)
Description: A two-week festival in Manitoba showcasing cultural diversity.
Experience: Traditional performances, cuisine, and global cultural immersion.
